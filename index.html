About Me








Want to know me?

I'm a student journalist and web editor for our school newspaper, the Epic, and a website developer/programmer.

In my free time, I love to play basketball with his friends, to watch random Netflix shows that pop up in my recommended list and play chess.

I am also part of several organizations that help underprivileged students in India. My passion for helping out underprivileged families started when I was seven-years-old. I visited a village, supported by an organization, AID India and loved interacting with the children and teaching them. So, I have worked with an organization called Association For India's Development (AID) for the past few years. Recently in the summer of 2020, my peers and I started TeenAIDers, a subchapter of AID Bay Area, an adult chapter of AID. I love working with my peers to provide better educational resources for children in India.

Other than my volunteer work, I love to learn about computers and develop my skills as a computer programmer because it allows people to get together to build something unique, share ideas, innovate, solve problems, and learn new things every day. I started to code about a year and a half ago, and since I was very passionate about it, I started doing it as a hobby, and in recent times, I have started to develop websites such as this, to grow my skills as a Front End developer.

Furthermore, being a student journalist has taught me higher level thinking skills, prepared me to deal with stress, gave me opportunities to work as a team, meet deadlines, problem solve, write, shoot and edit. I love to report important stories, to produce content that shines a light on our community and to break stories through diligent investigation and good reporting.
